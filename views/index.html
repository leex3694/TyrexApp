Notes

Overview
i) The purpose of this model is to estimate the financial implications of Cardiac Implantable Electronic Device (CIED) related infections with consideration of Medtronic's recently approved TYRX™ Absorbable Antibacterial Envelope, indicated to stabilize CIED placement and help prevent CIED infections. The model is designed to produce simple and transparent calculations customized for an individual hospital.
ii) Default values for healthcare utilization rates and costs are provided by Medtronic, based upon obtained from a study of 30 CIED related infections (Ahsan et al, 2014). These data can be customized at a country and hospital level with financial information from published literature or national and local estimates provided by robust analysis.
iii) The model assumes that TYRX™ will be used in patients at "high risk" of CIED infection. The selection of these patients, and the absolute risk of CIED infection in high risk patients, is determined by physicians. A default value is provided (3.6%) based on a study of TYRX™ (Mittal et al, 2014).
iv) The model uses a default value for the relative risk reduction in CIED infection, which is an average from two studies of TYRX™ (Mittal et al, 2014; Kolek et al, 2013).
V) The model uses a default value (can be modified) of 40% high risk patients, susceptible to CIED infections. This is an average found from a study published in American Heart Association, titled Rates of and Factors Associated with Infection in 200,909 Medicare Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator Implants: Results from the NCDR®” (Prutkin et al, 2014).
